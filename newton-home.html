Sir Isaac Newton (1643-1727)

"If I have seen further, it is by standing on the shoulders of Giants."

Born on Christmas Day 1643 in Woolsthorpe, England, Isaac Newton transformed our understanding of the physical world. His three laws of motion and universal gravitation laid the foundation for classical mechanics, while his work in optics revealed the composite nature of white light. Newton also independently developed calculus, providing mathematics with powerful new tools.
